About

My first brush with development was playing around with Age of Empires level editors to design a level and then later Lego Mindstorms, which took a very high level approach at programming. I did not realize it at the time, but I was hooked.

I took my first computer science class in high school and began learning java. However, I quickly realized that development is more than just writing code. In fact, I had already done some development without even realizing it... and I loved it.

After high school, I realized that the types of problems involved with software development were something I needed more of. This led me to study computer engineering at the University of Delaware. After graduation, I was hired as an Associate Applications Engineer at Silicon Labs. This was a great start, but I wanted to take on even more development.
